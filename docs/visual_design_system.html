Visual Design System

This is the single source of truth for all visual expressions of Heavenletters. It translates our brand essence of quiet connection into a tangible, consistent, and evocative visual language that honors Gloria Wendroff's legacy while creating a timeless digital sanctuary for spiritual seekers worldwide.

Purpose: This design system provides specific, directive guidance for visual identity designers, email campaign managers, ebook designers, web developers, and all team members working on Heavenletters touchpoints. Every design decision should flow from this document to ensure coherence across all expressions.

Design Principles

These principles guide every visual decision. They are non-negotiable anchors that ensure our design remains true to the Heavenletters essence.

Simplicity Over Spectacle

Prioritize clarity and calm. Avoid visual clutter, excessive decoration, or anything that distracts from the quiet connection we offer. Let the message breathe.

Warmth Over Hype

Every visual element should feel warm, inviting, and human. Avoid corporate polish without warmth, scarcity tactics, or aggressive marketing aesthetics.

Spacious & Rhythmic

Honor the relational space (Vā) between elements. Use generous margins, breathing room, and consistent rhythm. Design the in-between moments with intention.

Brand Essence

Quiet Connection

Heavenletters is a daily relational moment with God in plain speech—tender, courageous, unhurried. The core quality is felt more than named: a quiet coherence between message and life.

Guiding Metaphor: The sky at daybreak—boundless, gentle light, room for everyone.

Feeling Goals

What the community should reliably feel:

  • • Calm, nourished, nurtured
  • • Curious, energized, inspired
  • • Cared for, accompanied, never alone
  • • Dignified, trusted, welcomed

Emotional Palette

  • • Tempered joy, quiet confidence
  • • Humble boldness
  • • Spaciousness, breath, ease
  • • Soft radiance rather than glare

What to Avoid

Scarcity pressure, countdowns, urgency clocks, corporate polish without human warmth, jargon, hard-sell pricing talk, transactional framing, over-saturated palettes, harsh contrasts, dense typographic stacks, gamified UI tropes, and aggressive banners.

Color Usage Guidelines

  • Backgrounds: Use Cream (#f9f0d9) as the primary background. White for cards and content containers.
  • Text: Charcoal (#403f3e) for primary text. Use 70-80% opacity for secondary text.
  • Accents: Rose Primary for highlights and emotional emphasis. Sky Primary for calming accents. Gold Primary for divine/spiritual moments.
  • Contrast: Ensure WCAG AA compliance for all text. Test color combinations for accessibility.
  • Gradients: When using gradients, blend sky blues with golds or soft pinks to evoke sunrise/daybreak imagery.

Typography Guidelines

  • Line Height: Use 1.75 for body text, 1.3 for headings. Generous spacing creates calm and improves readability.
  • Line Length: Aim for 60-75 characters per line for optimal reading comfort.
  • Font Weights: Use Regular (400) for body, Semibold (600) for emphasis, Bold (700) for headings.
  • Letter Spacing: Default spacing is fine. Avoid tight tracking that reduces readability.
  • Heavenletter Content: Always use serif fonts (EB Garamond or Old Standard TT) for the actual Heavenletter text to honor the vintage, timeless quality.
  • UI Elements: Use sans-serif fonts (Lexend or Open Sans) for buttons, navigation, forms, and interface text.

Logo & Symbols

The Heavenletters visual identity is anchored by the pink rose symbol, representing divine love, beauty, and the gentle unfolding of spiritual connection. The logo combines this rose with angelic imagery and typography to create a complete brand mark.

Pink Rose Symbol

The heart of our visual identity. This pink rose represents divine love, gentle beauty, and the blossoming of spiritual connection. It is the primary symbol of Heavenletters.

Usage:

Use as the primary logo mark, favicon, social media profile image, and decorative element in headers and dividers.

Full Logo with Angel

The complete Heavenletters logo combining the angel illustration, pink rose, and typography. The tagline "Bringing Earth Closer to Heaven" expresses our mission.

Usage:

Use for website headers, email headers, printed materials, and official communications. Maintain clear space around the logo.

Favicon / Icon Mark

A simplified version combining the "H" monogram with the rose petals. Perfect for small-scale applications like favicons, app icons, and social media thumbnails.

Logo Usage Guidelines

  • Clear Space: Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the "H" in the logo around all sides.
  • Minimum Size: Never reproduce the full logo smaller than 120px wide for digital or 1 inch for print.
  • Color Variations: The logo can appear on cream, white, or light sky blue backgrounds. Avoid placing on busy or dark backgrounds.
  • Do Not: Distort, rotate, add effects, change colors, or separate elements of the logo.
  • Rose Symbol Alone: The pink rose can be used independently as a decorative element, icon, or watermark.

  • • Subtle overlays for email headers
  • • Ebook covers and chapter dividers
  • • Avoid heavy, stormy, or dramatic skies

Imagery Guidelines

All imagery used in Heavenletters materials should align with our brand essence of quiet connection, warmth, and spiritual accessibility. Images should feel spacious, natural, and human-scale.

✓ Do Use

  • • Spacious, natural landscapes (especially skies, clouds, horizons)
  • • Soft, natural lighting (dawn, daybreak, golden hour)
  • • Human-scale, authentic moments of quiet reflection
  • • Gentle, muted color palettes that align with our brand colors
  • • Vintage or timeless aesthetic (not overly modern or trendy)
  • • Images that evoke calm, nourishment, and spiritual peace
  • • Simple, uncluttered compositions with breathing room

✗ Avoid

  • • Over-saturated, harsh, or aggressive color palettes
  • • Stock photo clichés (overly posed, artificial smiles)
  • • Heavy religious symbolism (crosses, specific religious icons)
  • • Busy, cluttered, or chaotic compositions
  • • Dramatic, stormy, or dark imagery
  • • Corporate, sterile, or overly polished aesthetics
  • • Trendy filters or effects that date the imagery

Image Treatment & Effects

  • Overlays: When placing text over images, use a subtle gradient overlay (cream to transparent or sky blue to transparent) to ensure readability.
  • Opacity: Background images can be used at 20-40% opacity to create subtle texture without overwhelming content.
  • Borders & Frames: Use soft, rounded borders (8-12px radius) for images. Avoid sharp, harsh edges.
  • Aspect Ratios: Prefer 16:9 for hero images, 4:3 for cards, 1:1 for social media. Maintain consistency within each context.

Iconography

Icons should feel like gentle navigational tools—simple, clear, and warm. We use Feather Icons for their simplicity, consistency, and human warmth. Icons should never feel corporate or cold.

Icon Usage Guidelines

  • Style: Use line-based, stroke icons (not filled). Maintain consistent stroke weight (2px).
  • Size: Common sizes are 16px, 24px, 32px, 48px. Scale proportionally.
  • Color: Icons can be charcoal (default), or use brand colors (rose, sky, gold) for emphasis or category distinction.
  • Spacing: Provide adequate padding around icons. Never crowd them against text or edges.
  • Meaning: Use icons that have clear, universal meaning. Avoid obscure or ambiguous symbols.
  • Accessibility: Always pair icons with text labels or provide alt text. Never rely on icons alone to convey critical information.

Spacing & Layout

Generous spacing is fundamental to the Heavenletters aesthetic. We honor the relational space (Vā)—the fertile space between elements where connection and wisdom emerge. Use a 4px base unit for all spacing to create harmonious rhythm.

Spacing Scale

All margins, padding, and gaps should use multiples of 4px. This creates a subtle, consistent structure that makes the design feel intentional and calm.

Layout Principles

  • Roomy Margins: Use generous margins (48-64px on desktop, 24-32px on mobile) to create breathing room.
  • Content Width: Limit text content to 60-75 characters per line (roughly 700-800px max width) for optimal readability.
  • Vertical Rhythm: Maintain consistent spacing between sections (64-96px) to create a calm, predictable flow.
  • White Space: Embrace white (or cream) space. It's not empty—it's where the relational space lives.

Grid System

  • 12-Column Grid: Use a 12-column grid for flexible, responsive layouts.
  • Gutters: 24px gutters between columns on desktop, 16px on mobile.
  • Breakpoints: Mobile (<640px), Tablet (640-1024px), Desktop (>1024px).
  • Alignment: Prefer left-aligned text for body copy. Center alignment for headings and special moments only.

Applications

This design system applies across all Heavenletters touchpoints. Here are specific guidelines for key applications.

Email Campaigns

Visual Treatment

  • • Use cream (#f9f0d9) background
  • • Header with logo or rose symbol
  • • Generous white space (24-32px padding)
  • • Single column layout, max 600px wide
  • • Subtle sky imagery as header background (optional)

Typography & Content

  • • Serif fonts for Heavenletter content
  • • Sans-serif for UI elements and CTAs
  • • Line height 1.75 for body text
  • • One clear CTA button (rose or sky primary)
  • • Gentle P.S. for secondary message

Ebooks & PDFs

Cover Design

  • • Sky imagery or soft gradient background
  • • Pink rose symbol prominently featured
  • • Large serif title (EB Garamond or Old Standard TT)
  • • Gold accents for divine/spiritual emphasis
  • • Clean, uncluttered composition

Interior Layout

  • • Cream or white page backgrounds
  • • Serif fonts for all Heavenletter text
  • • Generous margins (1 inch minimum)
  • • Chapter dividers with rose or sky elements
  • • Page numbers in charcoal, subtle placement

Website Design

Visual Structure

  • • Cream background for main content areas
  • • White cards for individual Heavenletters
  • • Sky imagery in hero sections (subtle, 30% opacity)
  • • Sticky header with logo, minimal navigation
  • • Footer with rose symbol and essential links

Interaction & Motion

  • • Subtle hover states (slight color shift, no dramatic effects)
  • • Smooth transitions (200-300ms)
  • • Fades and dissolves over slides and spins
  • • Scroll animations: gentle, not jarring
  • • Focus states clearly visible for accessibility

Social Media Graphics

  • Profile Images: Use the pink rose symbol or favicon on cream or white background.
  • Post Graphics: Sky or soft gradient backgrounds, serif typography for quotes, rose or angel accents.
  • Dimensions: 1:1 for Instagram, 16:9 for Facebook/Twitter headers, 1080x1920 for Stories.
  • Consistency: Maintain brand colors and fonts across all social platforms.
